Ruda Zhang

Phillip Griffiths Assistant Research Professor

Duke University

My research combines machine learning (ML) and uncertainty quantification (UQ) for computational and data-enabled science and engineering (CDS&E).

I currently focus on manifold-based methods for dimension reduction and emulation of computational models. This includes learning manifold-valued mappings, where we adapt subspaces, bases, covariance matrices, or data matrices to model parameters. Another problem is to learn probability distributions on data manifolds, exploiting topological properties of data.

My previous work concerns the use of game theory and mechanism design in engineering and social systems, especially in urban transportation.
